At-Bat Baseball

A fully functional baseball-style simulation that utilizes 4, 6, 8, 10, 12, and 20 sided dice to determine the outcome of at-bats. The goal of the game is to have the most total bases by the 9th inning.

  • 12-sided die determines whether an at-bat results in a hit, walk, or out.
  • 20-sided die determines whether a hit is a single, double, triple, or homerun.
  • 4-sided die determines the type of out while the batting team hasn't hit for any total bases in the half-inning: strikeout, ground out, fly out, and line out.
  • 6-sided die determines the type of out while the batting team has hit for at least 1 total base in the half-inning strikeout, ground out, fly out, line out, and double play.
  • 8-sided die determines the type of out while the batting team has hit for at least 2 total bases in the half-inning: strikeout, ground out, fly out, line out, and double play.
  • 10-sided die determines the type of out while the batting team has hit for at least 3 total bases in the half-inning: strikeout, ground out, fly out, line out, double play, and triple play.
